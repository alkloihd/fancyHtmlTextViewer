üö∂‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏èWalking Animals

Walking means moving one foot at a time in a pattern.

Animals that walk:
üêé Horses - Walk with four legs
üêï Dogs - Walk and run on four paws
üêª Bears - Walk slowly on four big paws

ü¶òHopping Animals

Hopping means pushing off the ground with legs and landing again.

Animals that hop:
üê∞ Rabbits - Hop with strong back legs
üê∏ Frogs - Jump from lily pad to lily pad
ü¶ò Kangaroos - Can hop very far with each jump
üí° Did You Know?
A rabbit can hop 3 metres in one jump! That's longer than your classroom.

üèä‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏èSwimming Animals

Swimming means moving through water using fins, flippers, or webbed feet.

Animals that swim:
üêü Fish - Use fins and tail to swim
ü¶Ü Ducks - Use webbed feet like paddles
ü¶≠ Seals - Use flippers to swim fast
üí° Did You Know?

üïäÔ∏èFlying Animals

Flying means moving through the air using wings.

Animals that fly:
üê¶ Birds - Flap wings to fly
ü¶á Bats - Fly at night using wing membranes
ü¶ã Butterflies - Flutter colorful wings
üí° Did You Know?

üêçCrawling Animals

Crawling means moving close to the ground without legs.

Animals that crawl:
üêç Snakes - Slither in S-shaped curves
ü™± Worms - Crawl by stretching and shrinking
üêõ Caterpillars - Crawl using many tiny legs
üí° Did You Know?

üî§ Movement Vocabulary

Click on each word to see its meaning!

Walking
Moving by putting one foot in front of the other
Hopping
Moving by jumping with both feet together
Swimming
Moving through water using fins or webbed feet
Flying
Moving through the air using wings
Half-turn
Turning halfway around (180 degrees)
Quarter-turn
Turning one-fourth of the way around (90 degrees)
Direction
The way something moves (forward, backward, left, right)
Distance
How far something moves from one place to another
