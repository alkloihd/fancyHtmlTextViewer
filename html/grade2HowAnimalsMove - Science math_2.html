🐾 Moving Like Animals

Discover how different animals move through their world! Learn about walking, hopping, swimming, flying, and crawling while practising giving directions with special movement words.

🎯 Learning Goals & Success Criteria ▼

Learning Goals:

  • Describe and demonstrate various ways animals move in their environments

🐾 Types of Animal Movement

🚶‍♂️Walking Animals

Walking means moving one foot at a time in a pattern.

Animals that walk:
🐎 Horses - Walk with four legs
🐕 Dogs - Walk and run on four paws
🐻 Bears - Walk slowly on four big paws
💡 Did You Know?
A horse can walk about 4 kilometres in one hour! That's like walking from your school to the grocery store.

🦘Hopping Animals

Hopping means pushing off the ground with legs and landing again.

Animals that hop:
🐰 Rabbits - Hop with strong back legs
🐸 Frogs - Jump from lily pad to lily pad
🦘 Kangaroos - Can hop very far with each jump
💡 Did You Know?
A rabbit can hop 3 metres in one jump! That's longer than your classroom.

🏊‍♀️Swimming Animals

Swimming means moving through water using fins, flippers, or webbed feet.

Animals that swim:
🐟 Fish - Use fins and tail to swim
🦆 Ducks - Use webbed feet like paddles
🦭 Seals - Use flippers to swim fast
💡 Did You Know?
A duck's webbed feet work like swim fins! The skin between their toes helps them paddle.

🕊️Flying Animals

Flying means moving through the air using wings.

Animals that fly:
🐦 Birds - Flap wings to fly
🦇 Bats - Fly at night using wing membranes
🦋 Butterflies - Flutter colorful wings
💡 Did You Know?
A hummingbird can flap its wings 80 times in one second! That's faster than you can blink.

🐍Crawling Animals

Crawling means moving close to the ground without legs.

Animals that crawl:
🐍 Snakes - Slither in S-shaped curves
🪱 Worms - Crawl by stretching and shrinking
🐛 Caterpillars - Crawl using many tiny legs
💡 Did You Know?
A snake moves by pushing against the ground with its belly scales. It's like having tiny feet all along its body!

🔤 Movement Vocabulary

Click on each word to see its meaning!

Walking
Moving by putting one foot in front of the other
Hopping
Moving by jumping with both feet together
Swimming
Moving through water using fins or webbed feet
Flying
Moving through the air using wings
Half-turn
Turning halfway around (180 degrees)
Quarter-turn
Turning one-fourth of the way around (90 degrees)
Direction
The way something moves (forward, backward, left, right)
Distance
How far something moves from one place to another
